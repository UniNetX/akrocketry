## 1. What we learned from other Ardrey Kell club sites (browser review)

Visiting peer sites helps your site feel familiar to AK students and meet informal “club website” expectations on campus.

### 1.1 [AKICC on GitHub Pages](https://akhsicc.github.io/) (Inter-Club Council)

- **Visual language:** Strong Ardrey Kell identity—**knight imagery** and **purple** as the hero brand color.
- **Navigation pattern:** Short, predictable top nav: Home, All Clubs, Guidelines, Contact (ICC-specific, but the *principle* is clarity and low clutter).
- **Outgoing links:** Prominent link to schoolwide communications (e.g. Knightly News style bulletins)—you might mirror with a single line to **CMS school news** or a club GroupMe/Discord *after* advisor approval.
- **Social:** ICC points to **Instagram** (`@akhs.icc`). Your club should plan its **own** social handles and link them consistently in header/footer.

**Takeaway:** Align visually with AK where policy allows; keep navigation minimal and student-friendly.

### 1.2 [Ardrey Kell Math Club (Weebly)](https://akmathclub.weebly.com/)

- **Hero-first layout:** Large thematic image with **club name + one-line purpose** overlaid (“Official website for AK Math Club information”).
- **IA (information architecture):** Home, Meetings, Competitions, Resources, About, Contact.
- **Tone:** Academic but welcoming; clear buckets for “when we meet” vs “how we compete.”

**Takeaway:** Your site should open with a **strong visual + mission in one breath**, then route visitors to **schedule**, **competition**, and **resources** quickly.

### 1.3 [Ardrey Kell Biology Club (Weebly)](https://akbiologyclub.weebly.com/)

- **Governance visible:** Links to **club constitution** and **registration/intake forms** above the fold or in a dedicated area.
- **People section:** **Meet our officers** with names and roles (builds trust for parents/advisors).
- **IA:** Home, Schedule, Resources, **Compete** (competition-specific lane).

**Takeaway:** Post **officers**, **how to join**, and **rules/constitution** early—especially important for safety-forward clubs like rocketry.

### 1.4 [ICC “All Clubs” directory (Weebly)](https://akicc.weebly.com/all-clubs.html)

- **Directory behavior:** Clubs are grouped by **category** (Academic, Cultural, Service, Leisure, Social Justice, etc.). Many rows include **Website** and **About Video** links.
- **Implication:** Once recognized, you’ll want a **stable URL** and a **short “about” blurb** ICC can paste—or a **60–90s intro video** if you go that route.

**Takeaway:** Plan for a **one-paragraph club description** and a **single canonical link** you never break.

### 1.5 School and ICC expectations (high level)

From public ICC/school materials: recognized clubs are generally expected to keep **accurate, current information** available (often a **dedicated site** or **advisor-hosted page**). Exact wording changes year to year—**confirm with your advisor and ICC** before publishing anything that sounds “official.”

## 2. Purpose and audiences

### 2.1 Primary goals

1. **Recruitment:** Explain what the club does and how to join (zero prior experience welcome).
2. **Logistics:** When/where you meet; **launch** and **competition** dates; forms and fee structure if any.
3. **Safety & credibility:** Show that you follow **NAR/USAF MR** style safety thinking and school rules—parents and admins care about this.
4. **Outreach:** Promote the **Community House Middle School** basics program as its own program area (without overselling—it’s fine to say “coming soon” with a mailing list).
5. **Storytelling:** Photos and short updates from **American Rocketry Challenge (ARC)** and club **launches**.

### 2.2 Audiences

| Audience | What they need |
| -------- | -------------- |
| AK students | Cool factor, low friction join path, meeting schedule |
| Parents | Safety, supervision, costs, transportation, calendar |
| Advisors / admin | Policy alignment, contact, consistency with school branding rules |
| Middle school families | Clear scope of CHMS program, timing, signup |
| Sponsors / community | Professional tone, impact, how to help |
| ICC / directory maintainers | Short description + URL + category |

## 3. Programs you must represent (given facts)

### 3.1 American Rocketry Challenge — **two teams**

Plan distinct content blocks (not necessarily separate top-level nav items unless you want them):

- **Team A / Team B** names (even placeholders: “Team Phoenix / Team Vector”) once decided.
- **Season timeline:** Registration, design reviews, test launches, **qualification flights**, finals if applicable.
- **Roles:** Captains, leads for avionics/recovery, build, documentation, outreach.
- **Resources:** Link to **official ARC rules** (external), and your **internal** build standards (motor classes you fly, field checklists).
- **Photo policy:** Competition and build photos—**media releases** for minors if faces are visible.

### 3.2 Community House Middle School — **rocketry basics** (upcoming)

Treat as its own “program” with a lighter touch than high school ARC:

- **Audience:** Middle schoolers new to hobby rocketry.
- **Topics to list at a high level:** safety, parts of a rocket, stable flight, simple builds (e.g. Low Power), launch protocol, maybe career pathways.
- **Partnership:** Name partner teacher/admin at CHMS when confirmed; co-brand only with permission.
- **Signup:** Interest form + parent contact; clear “not a drop-off without registration” language if needed.

### 3.3 General club launches and events

- **Launch days:** Public-friendly description (what to bring, weather cancellations, spectator rules).
- **Non-ARC flights:** Educational launches, alumni demos, “community launch” days—good for photos and local press *if approved*.
- **Equipment:** Mention shared tools, rail/rod sizes, altimeters—helps returning members.

## 5. Page-by-page content plan

### 5.1 Home

- **Headline:** One sentence—what you are and who it’s for.
- **Subheads:** Three cards—**ARC**, **CHMS outreach**, **Launches**.
- **Proof points:** “Two ARC teams,” “Student-led, advisor-supported,” “Safety-first launches.”
- **Primary calls to action:** **Join**, **Calendar**, **CHMS interest** (as they become real).
- **News row:** Last 3 updates (even manual: “Next launch: …”, “ARC motor retainer arrived…”).

### 5.2 Join & Calendar

- **Onboarding steps:** Attend meeting → complete form → pay dues (if any) → safety acknowledgement.
- **Google Calendar or ICS:** Single source of truth; embed only if your platform supports it cleanly.
- **Conflicts:** Note AP exams, breaks, and **weather slip days**.

### 5.3 American Rocketry Challenge

- **Explain ARC in plain English** for freshmen/parents.
- **Team pages or tabs:** Roster (first name + last initial is OK for minors), roles, build gallery.
- **Ethics:** No posting confidential challenge details if rules prohibit; stick to **public** rulebook facts + your **own** designs’ non-sensitive photos.

### 5.4 Outreach (CHMS)

- **Positioning:** “High school club mentoring middle school introduction…”
- **Safety:** Always adult supervision, certified launches if applicable—wording vetted by advisors.
- **Forms:** Student interest + **parent/guardian** contact.

### 5.5 Launches & Safety

- **Pre-launch checklist** (simplified public version): range closed, pad clear, heads up, etc.
- **Motor matrix:** What you **do** and **do not** fly at school-sanctioned events.
- **Field locations:** Only after cleared for public listing (some fields shouldn’t be posted).

### 5.6 Resources

Curate, don’t dump links. Buckets:

- **Design / sim:** OpenRocket, basics of stability
- **Build:** adhesives, fin alignment, painting
- **Recovery:** chute sizing, shock cord, shear pins
- **Competition:** ARC official site + your team’s reading list
- **Safety:** NAR safety overview, first-time flyer tips

### 5.7 About / Leadership / Constitution

- **Officer roster** with roles and one fun line each (optional).
- **Advisor** block—name, role, how to reach through school channels.
- **Constitution PDF** + last-updated date.

### 5.8 Media / Gallery

- Thematic albums: **ARC build**, **Launch days**, **CHMS outreach**, **Competition travel**.
- Captions with **date + context**; no embarrassing shots of people without consent.

### 5.9 Sponsors & Support

- **Tiered recognition** if you do sponsorships: logo size guidelines.
- **Tax/donation language:** Only what your school foundation or PTA confirms.

### 5.10 Contact

- Prefer **club Gmail/Google group** or **school email** patterns your advisor approves.
- Link Instagram/TikTok/YouTube **only** if actively maintained.

## 6. Branding and design direction

### 6.1 School alignment

- **Colors:** Favor AK **purple** + neutral grays/white; use accent color for “rocketry” (orange flame, electric blue trajectory—pick one and stay consistent).
- **Logo:** If you use official AK marks, **get approval** from school administration. Many schools restrict logo use; a **club wordmark** (“AK Rocketry Club” + simple rocket icon you have rights to) is often safer.
- **Tone:** Confident, inclusive, safety-conscious—not reckless slogans.

### 6.2 Layout patterns borrowed from AK sites

- **Large hero** (Math Club style) with overlay text.
- **Short top navigation** (ICC style: few items, no dropdown maze).
- **Officers + constitution** near top of About (Biology Club style).

### 6.3 Accessibility (non-negotiable)

- Sufficient **contrast** (purple text on white OK; thin low-contrast purple on photos may fail).
- **Alt text** for all meaningful images.
- **Headings in order** (one H1 per page).
- **Forms** labeled; error messages readable.
- **Motion:** avoid autoplay video with sound; respect `prefers-reduced-motion` if you build custom.

## 7. Trust, safety, and legal (planning checklist)

Work with your advisor on every item before publishing.

- **Photos of minors:** CMS/media release expectations.
- **Location privacy:** Home addresses; precise field GPS if sensitive.
- **Assumption of risk:** School may supply boilerplate for athletics-like activities—ask.
- **Vendor liability:** You describe club activities; you don’t speak for Estes, Apogee, etc.
- **ARC IP:** Follow competition media and design disclosure rules each season.

## 8. Technology options (planning only—no code)

| Option | Pros | Cons |
| ------ | ---- | ---- |
| **Weebly** (like many AK clubs) | Fast, familiar to peers, easy forms | Less flexible design; ads/branding on free tier |
| **GitHub Pages** (like new ICC site) | Free, versioned, customizable | Needs someone comfortable with git/deploy |
| **Google Sites** | Very easy, school Google integration | Limited design polish |
| **Notion / Linked doc** | Ultra-fast MVP | Weak for gallery and SEO; looks less “official” |

**Recommendation for MVP:** Match ecosystem (**Weebly or GitHub Pages**) based on who will maintain the site for **three years**. If only you know git, use Weebly or Google Sites and document passwords for the next officers.
